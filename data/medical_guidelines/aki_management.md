# Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) Management

## KDIGO Staging
- **Stage 1**: Creatinine 1.5-1.9x baseline OR increase ≥0.3 mg/dL within 48 hours OR UOP <0.5 mL/kg/hr × 6-12 hours
- **Stage 2**: Creatinine 2.0-2.9x baseline OR UOP <0.5 mL/kg/hr × ≥12 hours
- **Stage 3**: Creatinine ≥3.0x baseline OR increase to ≥4.0 mg/dL OR initiation of RRT OR UOP <0.3 mL/kg/hr × ≥24 hours OR anuria ≥12 hours

## Etiology — Prerenal (60-70%)
- Volume depletion: Hemorrhage, GI losses, burns, diuresis
- Decreased effective circulating volume: Heart failure, cirrhosis, sepsis
- Hemodynamic: NSAIDs (afferent arteriole), ACEi/ARBs (efferent arteriole)
- Diagnostic clues: BUN/Cr ratio >20:1, FENa <1%, FEUrea <35%, bland sediment

## Etiology — Intrinsic Renal (25-30%)
- **Acute tubular necrosis (ATN)**: Ischemia or nephrotoxins (contrast, aminoglycosides, cisplatin)
- **Acute interstitial nephritis (AIN)**: Drug-induced (NSAIDs, antibiotics, PPIs), autoimmune
- **Glomerulonephritis**: Rapidly progressive GN — urgent evaluation (ANCA, anti-GBM, complement levels)
- Diagnostic clues: FENa >2%, muddy brown casts (ATN), WBC casts (AIN), RBC casts (GN)

## Etiology — Postrenal (5-10%)
- Obstruction: BPH, nephrolithiasis, malignancy, blood clots
- Diagnosis: Renal ultrasound showing hydronephrosis
- Treatment: Urgent decompression (Foley catheter, ureteral stent, nephrostomy tube)

## Management Principles
- **Volume assessment**: JVP, lung exam, edema, orthostatics; use point-of-care ultrasound
- **Prerenal**: IV fluid resuscitation (isotonic crystalloid); discontinue offending agents
- **Avoid nephrotoxins**: Stop NSAIDs, aminoglycosides, contrast; hold ACEi/ARB temporarily
- **Monitor**: Creatinine and electrolytes at least daily; strict I&O; daily weights
- **Medication dose adjustments**: Renally cleared drugs require adjustment

## Indications for Dialysis (AEIOU)
- **A**cidosis: Severe metabolic acidosis (pH <7.1) refractory to bicarbonate
- **E**lectrolytes: Hyperkalemia refractory to medical management
- **I**ntoxication: Toxic alcohols, lithium, salicylates
- **O**verload: Volume overload refractory to diuretics
- **U**remia: Uremic encephalopathy, pericarditis, bleeding

## Prevention
- Adequate hydration before contrast procedures
- Avoid nephrotoxic drug combinations
- Monitor renal function with high-risk medications
- Volume resuscitation in sepsis per Surviving Sepsis guidelines
