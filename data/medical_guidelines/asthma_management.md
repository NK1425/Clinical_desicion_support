# Asthma Stepwise Management (Adults and Adolescents ≥12 years)

## Diagnosis
- Variable respiratory symptoms: Wheeze, shortness of breath, chest tightness, cough
- Variable expiratory airflow limitation: FEV1/FVC reduced, positive bronchodilator reversibility (≥12% and ≥200mL increase in FEV1)
- Supportive: Peak flow variability >10%, positive methacholine challenge, FeNO ≥25 ppb

## Stepwise Therapy (GINA 2024)
### Step 1 — Mild Intermittent
- **Preferred**: As-needed low-dose ICS-formoterol (budesonide-formoterol)
- **Alternative**: As-needed SABA + ICS taken together

### Step 2 — Mild Persistent
- **Preferred**: Low-dose ICS daily OR as-needed low-dose ICS-formoterol
- **Alternative**: LTRA (montelukast) — less effective than ICS

### Step 3 — Moderate Persistent
- **Preferred**: Low-dose ICS-LABA maintenance (with as-needed ICS-formoterol)
- **Alternative**: Medium-dose ICS, or low-dose ICS + LTRA

### Step 4 — Moderate-Severe
- **Preferred**: Medium-dose ICS-LABA (with as-needed ICS-formoterol as reliever)
- **Alternative**: Add tiotropium (Spiriva Respimat), or high-dose ICS-LABA

### Step 5 — Severe
- **Preferred**: High-dose ICS-LABA + LAMA (tiotropium)
- **Add-on**: Anti-IgE (omalizumab), anti-IL5/IL5R (mepolizumab, benralizumab), anti-IL4R (dupilumab), anti-TSLP (tezepelumab)
- Low-dose oral corticosteroids as last resort
- Phenotype-guided biologic selection based on eosinophils, IgE, FeNO

## Asthma Control Assessment
Assess over last 4 weeks:
- Daytime symptoms >2/week
- Any night waking due to asthma
- Reliever needed >2/week
- Any activity limitation
- **Well controlled**: None of the above; **Partly**: 1-2; **Uncontrolled**: 3-4

## Acute Exacerbation Management
- **Mild-Moderate**: SABA 4-10 puffs via MDI + spacer every 20 min × 1 hour; prednis(ol)one 40-50mg × 5-7 days
- **Severe**: Continuous nebulized SABA, ipratropium bromide, systemic corticosteroids, supplemental O2 (target 93-95%)
- **Life-threatening**: IV magnesium sulfate 2g over 20 min, consider IV salbutamol, ICU admission, intubation if needed

## Asthma Action Plan
- Green zone (well): Continue maintenance medications
- Yellow zone (worsening): Increase ICS dose or start ICS-formoterol reliever, add OCS if not improving
- Red zone (emergency): Seek immediate medical care, use SABA, start OCS
