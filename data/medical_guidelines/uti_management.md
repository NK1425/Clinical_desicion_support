# Urinary Tract Infection (UTI) Management

## Classification
- **Uncomplicated cystitis**: Lower UTI in non-pregnant women without structural abnormalities
- **Complicated UTI**: UTI with structural/functional abnormality, male sex, pregnancy, immunosuppression, indwelling catheter
- **Pyelonephritis**: Upper UTI involving renal parenchyma
- **Catheter-associated UTI (CAUTI)**: Symptoms + culture ≥1000 CFU/mL with catheter in place within 48 hours

## Diagnosis
- Symptoms: Dysuria, frequency, urgency, suprapubic pain (cystitis); fever, flank pain, CVA tenderness (pyelonephritis)
- Urinalysis: Pyuria (>10 WBC/HPF), positive leukocyte esterase, nitrites (Gram-negative bacteria)
- Culture: ≥100,000 CFU/mL (symptomatic: ≥1000 CFU/mL may be significant)

## Uncomplicated Cystitis — First-Line Treatment
- **Nitrofurantoin** 100mg BID × 5 days (avoid if CrCl <30)
- **TMP-SMX** 160/800mg BID × 3 days (if local resistance <20%)
- **Fosfomycin** 3g single dose (less effective than above)
- **Avoid** fluoroquinolones for uncomplicated cystitis (reserve for complicated infections)

## Pyelonephritis
### Outpatient (mild, no nausea/vomiting)
- **Ciprofloxacin** 500mg BID × 7 days, or **Levofloxacin** 750mg daily × 5 days
- **TMP-SMX** 160/800mg BID × 14 days (if susceptible)
- Consider single dose IM ceftriaxone 1g before starting oral therapy

### Inpatient (severe, sepsis, unable to tolerate PO)
- **Ceftriaxone** 1g IV daily, or **Ciprofloxacin** 400mg IV BID
- **Piperacillin-tazobactam** or **Meropenem** if ESBL suspected
- Switch to PO when afebrile ≥48 hours and tolerating PO

## Complicated UTI
- Obtain culture before starting empiric therapy
- Broader spectrum: Fluoroquinolone, ceftriaxone, or piperacillin-tazobactam
- Duration: 7-14 days depending on severity and response
- Address underlying cause (obstruction, catheter removal/exchange)

## Special Populations
- **Pregnancy**: Treat asymptomatic bacteriuria; safe antibiotics: amoxicillin, cephalexin, nitrofurantoin (avoid near term)
- **Men**: Always consider complicated; evaluate for prostatitis; duration typically 7-14 days
- **Recurrent UTI** (≥3/year): Consider prophylaxis — low-dose nitrofurantoin, post-coital prophylaxis, vaginal estrogen (postmenopausal)
